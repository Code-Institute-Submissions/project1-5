The Monkees

History

The Monkees are an American rock and pop band formed in Los Angeles in 1965. Originally they were four actor-musicians for the American television series "The Monkees" (1966 - 1968). The band was integrated by Americans Micky Dolenz, Michael Nesmith, and Peter Tork; and the British actor and singer Davy Jones.

Music

Clarksville (1966)

The song was written by Tommy Boyce and Bobby Hart. The lyrics tell of a man phoning the woman he loves, urging her to meet him at a train station in Clarksville before he must leave, possibly forever.

I am a Believer (1966)

It is a song composed by Neil Diamond.The song was No. 1 in the UK Singles Chart for four weeks in January and February 1967 and reached the top spot in numerous countries, including Australia, New Zealand, Canada, and Ireland.

Stepping Stone (1966)

It is a song written by Tommy Boyce and Bobby Hart. The song is simple musically, with a repeating verse chord progression of E major, G major, A major, and C major, and a repeating bridge in cut time of E major, G major, A major, and G major.

Daydream Believer (1967)

The song composed by John Stewart. The soong is about Married couples that start out in an idealistic haze, but after a few years it wears off, and each sees the other as he or she really is. This is, supposedly, when genuine love is proven.
